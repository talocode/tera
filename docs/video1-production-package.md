### [1:45–3:15] STEP 1: UNDERSTAND THE TOPIC

**On screen:** Screen recording — open teraai.chat, sign in with Google.

**Voiceover:**
"Step one: actually understand the topic. Go to teraai.chat and sign in. You get 150 free credits per month — that's enough for serious studying. No credit card needed."

**On screen:** Type into Tera chat:

```
Explain photosynthesis like I'm 10 years old. Use a simple analogy a kid would understand, then give me 3 real-world examples.
```

**Voiceover:**
"Here's the trick — don't just say 'explain photosynthesis.' Give Tera a specific audience. 'Explain it like I'm 10.' 'Use an analogy.' This forces it to simplify instead of dumping textbook jargon on you."

**On screen:** Show Tera's response. Pause on the analogy. Highlight it with cursor.

**Voiceover:**
"Look at that analogy. Plants are like tiny food factories — sunlight is the power, water and CO2 are the ingredients, and sugar is the product. That's something you'll actually remember. Way better than memorizing 'chloroplasts convert light energy into chemical energy.'"

**On screen:** Type:

```

```

**Voiceover:**
"Now ask for real-world examples. This connects the abstract concept to things you already know. That's how memory works — new information sticks when it attaches to something familiar."

### [3:15–5:00] STEP 2: TEST YOURSELF

**On screen:** Type into Tera:

```
Create 5 quiz questions about photosynthesis. Mix multiple choice and short answer. Include the answers at the end.
```

**Voiceover:**
"Step two: test yourself. This is the most important part. Reading feels productive, but it's not. Testing is where learning actually happens. It's called the testing effect — the act of trying to recall information strengthens your memory more than reading the same thing five times."

**On screen:** Show Tera generating quiz questions. Scroll through them slowly.

**Voiceover:**
"Tera generates five questions from the topic you just studied. Multiple choice and short answer. Try answering each one before you look at the answer. If you get it wrong, that's good — it means you found a gap."

**On screen:** Type:

```
I got questions 2 and 4 wrong. Explain why those answers are correct and give me a memory trick to remember them.
```

**Voiceover:**
"And here's where it gets powerful — tell Tera which ones you got wrong. It'll explain the correct answers and even give you memory tricks. That's personalized tutoring. For free."

```

**Voiceover:**
"Step three: build a study plan. This is where most students fail — not because they don't study, but because they don't have a plan. They cram the night before and forget everything."

**On screen:** Show Tera generating the 7-day plan. Scroll through each day.

**Voiceover:**
"Tera builds you a realistic 7-day plan. One hour per day. It mixes different types of studying — reading, practice questions, review. And it accounts for the fact that you have school during the day. This is way better than 'study everything the night before.'"

**On screen:** Type:

```
Can you make day 3 more focused on genetics? That's my weakest area.
```

**Voiceover:**
"And you can customize it. Tell Tera which days need more focus on your weak areas. It adjusts the plan for you."

### [6:30–7:45] STEP 4: SUMMARIZE YOUR NOTES

**On screen:** Type into Tera:

```
I have notes on photosynthesis. Summarize them into 5 key points I need to remember for the exam. Format as bullet points.
```

**Voiceover:**
"Step four: summarize. If you have pages of notes, you don't need to reread all of them. Ask Tera to pull out the key points. Five things you absolutely need to remember."

**On screen:** Show Tera's summary. Highlight the 5 bullet points.

**Voiceover:**
"Now you have a focused summary. These five points are what you review the day before the exam. Not 20 pages. Just the essentials."

Turn these 5 points into flashcards with the concept on one side and the explanation on the other.
```

**Voiceover:**
"Bonus — ask Tera to turn those points into flashcards. You can use these for spaced repetition throughout the week."

**Voiceover:**

The three things that changed for me: I understood concepts faster with personalized explanations. I retained more because I tested myself. And I studied more consistently because I had a plan instead of cramming.

Is it perfect? No. Sometimes Tera gives explanations that are too simple or too complex. You have to adjust. But for a free tool that's available 24/7, it's the best study partner I've found."

## Production Notes

- **Don't overpromise.** The video shows what Tera can do, not what it can't. No "this will change your life" — just "this helped me study better."

- **The CTA is soft.** "Try it yourself" not "sign up now." Match the educational tone.
